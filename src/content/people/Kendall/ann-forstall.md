## Research Notes

### Surname question
- Three spelling variants attested: "Fostall" (FamilySearch), "Forstall" (Geni), "Fosball" (FamilySearch marriage index). All likely the same name.
- The hamlet of Forstal sits just north of Wingham village — the surname almost certainly derives from this place ("at the Forstal" → medieval "atte Forstall"). A 1818 Kent history indexes "Wm. Atte Forstall" confirming the name existed in the Wingham area.
- **Zero results** for Forstall or Fostall in FreeBMD civil registration (all of England, 1837 onward). The surname effectively did not survive into the Victorian era. Ann may have been the last generation to carry it.
- Son Frederick Fostall Kendall (1827) confirms it was treated as a real family surname, not just a place description — they preserved it as a middle name.

### Parents
- FamilySearch tree lists parents as Henry (M) and Martha (F) — **no surnames given**. Source unverified. The "Forstall" surname may have been applied to the parents by tree contributors, not from primary records.
- Christening date of 27 January 1793 is specific enough to locate in the Wingham St Mary parish register — but images are **restricted access** (FamilySearch center or affiliate library only).
