# Emil Mielke

**Lifespan:** April 29, 1881 – April 20, 1970

Born April 29, 1881 in the **town of Sturgeon Bay** to **Fred Meikle and Mary (Johnsen) Meikle** (parents per FindAGrave, now corroborated by Emil's own 1970 obituary: "Born Apr. 29, 1881 in Sturgeon Bay town to Fred and Mary Meikle"). Farmer; lifelong member of **Shiloh Moravian church**. Father of Hazel P. Mielke (1902–1987). Married Margaret Pfister (1876–1962, b. Fish Creek, Door Co, WI) on **October 16, 1902** — the marriage record renders her surname "Pfisterer" (WHS index MR2175482: "Pfister, Magart," Oct 16, 1902, Door, Vol 2, p. 243, seq 04639). **Divorced from Margaret before 1930** (per 1930 census), then married Tillie H. Karnopp (1883–1967) **in 1929** (per 1970 obituary). Siblings: **Ernest** (Green Bay, 1970), **Oscar** (Chicago, 1970), **Iva (Mrs. Linquist)** (California, 1970). Died April 20, 1970 at home, 454 N. 7th Ave., Sturgeon Bay, after six months' illness, age 88. Buried at Bayside Cemetery, Sturgeon Bay, Door County, Wisconsin.

**Ethnicity RESOLVED (2026-07-15): the paternal line is Scottish — Meikle, not Mielke.** See Research Notes.

## Research Notes
- Surname variants: Mielke (German), Meikle (Find A Grave + parents' surname), Miekle (Hazel's obituary).
- **Ethnicity ambiguity — RESOLVED 2026-07-15: Scottish.** The 2026-06-10 hypothesis was right. See "Research target — RESOLVED" section below for the full evidence chain (William Meikle b. 1813 Glasgow, the Bayside cluster, and the confirming negatives).
- Buried at Bayside Cemetery — same cemetery as daughter Hazel and son-in-law Clarence Lau.
- Lived to 88 — died 9 days before his 89th birthday.
- Sturgeon Bay, Door County is the family's base — Emil, Hazel, and Earl all associated with Sturgeon Bay.
- **Hazel's pre-marital birth:** Hazel was born **July 12, 1902** — three months *before* her parents' October 16, 1902 marriage. Three plausible readings: (a) Hazel was born to Margaret out of wedlock and Emil & Margaret legitimized the relationship that fall; (b) one of the two dates is off in the records; (c) Hazel was Emil's child by an earlier relationship that Margaret then raised. Catholic Door County would make (a) common-but-quietly-handled. Worth a single targeted check of Hazel's actual birth/baptism record if it ever falls into your lap, but per stop rule, don't go hunting.
- **Divorce as story shift:** the previous record framed Emil as "widowed by Margaret in 1962, then remarried Tillie" — a tidy late-life remarriage. The actual story is messier: married Oct 1902, divorced before 1930 (so by their late 40s), then both remarried separately. Margaret kept the Mielke name. All three (Emil, Margaret, Tillie) are buried in the same Bayside Cemetery in Sturgeon Bay despite the divorce — small-town Door County reality.
- **Ruled-out candidate (do not re-investigate):** **Fredrick D. Mielke (1877–1959)**, son of **Johann Mielke + Wilhelmine Mielke** (born Germany), surfaced as a possible sibling because his sibling list includes "Emil Mielke." **Not our Emil** — different parents (Fred Meikle + Mary Johnsen, not Johann + Wilhelmine), different surname-spelling pattern (consistently Mielke, not the Meikle/Miekle drift), different children's names. Mielke is one of the most common German surnames; multiple unrelated Emil Mielkes existed in late-1800s Wisconsin. Logged here so this candidate doesn't get re-chased in future sessions.

## Research target — Mielke (German) vs. Meikle (Scottish) ethnicity — RESOLVED SCOTTISH [2026-07-15]
**Answer: the paternal surname line is Scottish.** Stop rule lifted by Foggy 2026-07-15; deep-dive session (WHS index + Door County Library newspaper archive + FindAGrave) resolved it:

- **The Sturgeon Bay Meikle patriarch is William Meikle, b. Feb 6, 1813 near Glasgow, Scotland; emigrated 1848** with his family to New York state, then Ontario, Canada; died May 1, 1893 in Sturgeon Bay at son Alex's home, nine days after arriving from Chesley, Ontario. Obituary: *The Democrat*, May 4, 1893, p. 1 ("OBITUARY MENTION — Wm. Meikle Died Only a Few Days After His Arrival In This City"). Buried Bayside, Ward B (FindAGrave #130602798).
- **William's documented sons (all Bayside):** Alexander (b. Nov 18, 1839 Scotland, d. Apr 5, 1914 — timber expert, Gardner justice of the peace, Sturgeon Bay alderman; FG #203230000; obit Door County Democrat Apr 10, 1914, p. 1; longer obit clipped in Wisconsin Necrology Vol. 14, pp. 42–43 at WHS); James (b. 1841 Scotland, d. 1917 — Civil War veteran; m. **Adelaide PINNEY**, d. Apr 30/May 1, 1882, leaving four children — she was the Genealogy Trails obituary lead, now identified; FG #130602683/#130602618; obit Door County Democrat Jun 22, 1917, p. 1); John (1845–1931, FG #130602609).
- **Fred's place in this family is probable but UNPROVEN** — FindAGrave links only Alexander, James, and John as William's children. Fred (Emil's father) is the same surname, same town, same cemetery cluster, right generation — but no record yet names his parents. See fred-meikle.md for the proof path (the Oct 1902 marriage record and 1900 census are the two one-shot answers).
- **Confirming negatives:** zero Mielke-spelled records in Door County in the WHS pre-1907 index (432 statewide, none Door); zero "Emil Mielke" hits in 117 years of Door County newspapers. The archive's "Mielke" standardization is backwards — **the family spelling is Meikle**; "Mielke" is the drift, from German-dominated Green Bay/record contexts (Hazel's 1987 obit "Miekle" etc.).
- **Still open — the maternal half:** Mary **Johnsen** (Scandinavian form) + Emil's lifelong membership in **Shiloh/Sturgeon Bay Moravian church** (a historically Norwegian congregation there) still points to a Norwegian mother. Unproven; the 1902 marriage record or 1900 census would state her birthplace.
