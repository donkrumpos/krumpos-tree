# Fred Meikle

**Emil Mielke's father — Don's 3x-great-grandfather.** File created 2026-07-15 when the stop rule was lifted and the Meikle deep-dive ran.

## Summary
Farmer in the Sturgeon Bay area, Door County, Wisconsin. Married Mary (Johnsen). At least four children: Emil (b. Apr 29, 1881, town of Sturgeon Bay), Ernest, Oscar, and Iva. Documented in the Fish Creek/Egg Harbor area 1899 and in Sawyer (west Sturgeon Bay) 1917. **Probable son of William Meikle (b. Feb 6, 1813 near Glasgow, Scotland; emigrated 1848; d. May 1, 1893 Sturgeon Bay)** — same rare surname, same town, same Bayside Cemetery family cluster, right generation — but no record yet names Fred's parents.

## Relationships
- **Wife:** Mary (Johnsen) Meikle — per FindAGrave (Emil's memorial) and Emil's 1970 obituary ("Fred and Mary Meikle"). **Johnsen** is a Scandinavian form; combined with the family's Sturgeon Bay Moravian membership (a historically Norwegian congregation), a Norwegian origin is suspected — unproven. A "Mary Meikle" appears as a Ward Three Sturgeon Bay property holder in a 1918 tax-sale legal notice (Door County Democrat, May 31, 1918) — candidate for Mary as widow.
- **Children:** Emil (1881–1970, direct line), Ernest (Green Bay, 1970), Oscar (Chicago, 1970), Iva (Mrs. Linquist, California, 1970) — siblings per Emil's 1970 obituary. WHS index birth "Unnamed Female Meikle, Aug 30, 1890, Door County" is a candidate for Iva.
- **Probable father:** William Meikle (1813–1893) — see emil-mielke.md "RESOLVED" section for the full cluster. William's FindAGrave links only Alexander (1839–1914), James (1841–1917), and John (1845–1931) as children; Fred is unlinked.
- **Probable brothers:** Alexander, James (m. Adelaide Pinney, d. 1882), John — all Sturgeon Bay, all Bayside Cemetery.
