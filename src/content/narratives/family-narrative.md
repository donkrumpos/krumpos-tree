## Krumpos Line

**Lawrence Krumpos** — patriarch, two marriages. First to **Frances (Kosnar)** (details sparse; obituary never found in digitized records). Second to **Mathilda (Henrietta) Schneider** (b. ~1897, Kenosha County; d. 1973, De Pere).

Great-great-grandparents: **Joseph Krumpos** m. **Mary (Steffel) Krumpos**.

### First Marriage Children (Frances Kosnar)
- **Kenneth L. Krumpos** (1912–2001) — Born in Lena, Oconto County. Personnel manager at Procter & Gamble for 42 years. WWII Italy: Victory Medal, EAME Ribbon with 3 Bronze Battle Stars, Good Conduct Medal.
- **Harold Krumpos** — married Margaret; details sparse.
- **Lester F. Krumpos** (1918–1944) — killed in WWII; Silver Star.

### Second Marriage Children (Mathilda Schneider)
- **Dorothy L. Krumpos** (1923–2011) — m. Leo D. Matuszak (Sept 5, 1942). Stay-at-home mother of six sons. Den Mother/Den Leader Coach, Troop Secretary 1965–1974 for Cub/Boy Scout Pack 113 at St. John the Baptist Church, Howard.
- **Donald Howard Krumpos** (1924–1964) — WWII Navy (ARM2). Died young at 39. Married Dorothy Elaine Coppersmith (Sept 27, 1947). Foggy's grandfather.
- **Jerome Marvin Krumpos** (1928–1950) — killed in Korea; Purple Heart.
- **Lawrence "Larry" Krumpos** (1930–2013) — Korean War veteran. Owned Larry's De Pere Ambulance Service. Worked James River Corporation 42 years. Coached baseball and football 37 years in De Pere. *"Heart of gold... always the first to volunteer."*
- **Lloyd**, **Rosemary** (m. Don Koenig, Auburndale FL), **Mae** (m. Willems, De Pere).

### Donald H. + Dorothy Elaine (Coppersmith) Krumpos
Children: **Jim** (m. Cindy → Jim Jr., Don, Helen), **Keith** (m. Xiao → Bridget), **Steven**, **Debra** (m. Bob Cook → Kevin, Brian), **Kim** (m. Bob Younkle → Megan, Courtney), **Carol** (m. Michael Smith → Rachel, Kelsey), **Catherine** (1952–1965, died young).

**Don (Donald A.) Krumpos** — Foggy. Son of Jim Krumpos. Married Jessica.

## Coppersmith Line

**Claude Coppersmith** (1907–1987) m. **Marie Bodoh** (1906–1998). Married June 22, 1926, Green Bay. Claude was son of **Frank Joseph Coppersmith** and **Odile (Clemens) Barette**. Marie likely daughter of **John Bodoh** and **Mary Young** (unconfirmed; Foggy has a 5-generation photograph of Mary Young).

### Children
- **Dorothy Elaine Coppersmith** (1928–2002) — m. Donald H. Krumpos. Library secretary at Green Bay Public Elementary Schools for 20 years. Widowed at 35, raised 7 children through multiple tragedies including daughter Catherine's death (1965). *"She was a truly selfless human being... determined to carry on for her children and in so doing, taught us the value in being content in all things."* Died from Huntington's Chorea.
- **Leonard**, **Ronald** — Green Bay.
- **Roger H. Coppersmith** (1943–2018) — US Army; 40+ years at Georgia Pacific.
- **Robert** — deceased.
- **Florence (Coppersmith) Challe** (1932–2020) — m. Alton Challe (March 3, 1951, Lawton OK). Founded Challe Real Estate and Property Management in Oregon area; ran 35 years. *"A prayer-warrior, seen often with a rosary in her hand."*

## Martin / Schmidt Line

**Clifford Alfred Schmidt** (1918–1991) — born Cleveland MN; died Crooked Lake WI. Son of **Fred J. Schmidt** and **Mary J. Kendall**. Married **Helen M. Martin** in 1944.

**Helen M. Martin** (1924–1974) — born Sturgeon Bay. Daughter of **Gerald Martin Sr.** and **Hazel P. Mielke**. Gerald died May 1931 when Helen was 6. Hazel later married Clarence Lau (May 7, 1932; d. Aug 1985).

### Children
- **Betty Mae (Schmidt) Krumpos** (1947–1993) — married Keith Krumpos. Foggy's mother. Died at 46.
- **Ken Schmidt** (1945–2021) — Foggy's godfather. US Army Reserves. Crown and bridge technician. Played banjo and guitar. Loved birding, carving birds, gardening — *"his raspberry patch was legendary with the grandkids."* m. Cathy (Thorp). *"Per Ken's wishes, there will not be a funeral."*

**Hazel P. Mielke** (Jul 12, 1902–Apr 8, 1987) — born Sturgeon Bay. Daughter of **Emil Mielke** and **Margaret Mielke**. Siblings: Earl Mielke, Mildred (Gritzmacher), Pearl (Kline). Buried Bayside Cemetery, Sturgeon Bay.
